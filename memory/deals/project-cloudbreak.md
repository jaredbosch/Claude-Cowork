## Key Intel / Flags

- **Occupancy story is the deal** — 65% wtd avg with massive vacancy in ND (Wheatland 30%, Magic City 34%, Larimore 38%). JLL is pitching this as upside; real risk is why it's so vacant.
- **POH heavy** — 581 park-owned homes, majority in ND. JLL pitches sell-off strategy but managing/selling 581 homes is a significant operational lift for a buyer.
- **ND in-place rents above market** at Holiday ($938 vs $650 mkt) and Jefferson ($777 vs $650 mkt) — worth scrutinising. Could be different product, could be over-market.
- **Idaho is Moscow-heavy** — 7 of 10 assets in one college town (University of Idaho). Market rent $725 across all of them; limited rent growth upside.
- **Vertically integrated platform included** — seller has 27-year operating platform (property mgmt, home retail, financing, back-office). Buyer essentially acquires an operating business, not just assets.
- **No rent control** in ID, MT, or ND — clean regulatory story.
- **JLL framing** — "one of the largest single-owner MHC portfolios in the US", "immediate regional scale." Pitched hard at institutional/REIT buyers.
- **Expense ratio concerns** — T-6 actual shows 27.6% expense ratio (very low), but JLL underwrites to 42.3%. Big gap. Buyer will need to stress-test.
