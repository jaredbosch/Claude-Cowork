# Communication Style

## Professional Writing — General Principles
- **Fact-first.** Financial data, deal specifics, and seller anecdotes are the source of truth. Everything else exists to support those facts — not to fill space.
- **Direct and polished.** No fluff, no throat-clearing. Get to the point quickly.
- **Tone scales with relationship.** Closer client relationships = less formal. New/institutional contacts = clean and professional.
- **No corporate speak.** Avoid filler phrases, vague language, and buzzwords. If it doesn't add information, cut it.

## Client Emails
- Direct and fact-based
- Polished but not stiff
- Warmer/less formal with established relationships
- Lead with the relevant fact or ask — not a pleasantry
- Anti-patterns: "Hope this finds you well", "Please don't hesitate to reach out", "As per my previous email", "Leverage" as a verb

## BOV / Pitch Writing (narrative structure)
1. **Sunstone intro** — who we are, why we're the right firm
2. **Credentials** — relevant experience, track record, comparable deals closed
3. **Property specifics** — the deal, the opportunity, the numbers
4. **Comparable data** — similar deals, market evidence that supports the valuation
5. **Close** — why Sunstone wins this assignment

Tone: more narrative and persuasive than an OM, but still grounded in data and precedent.

## OM / Marketing Writing
- Clean, precise, institutional quality
- Data-forward: tables, financials, rent comps, sales comps, market stats
- Structure: Property Info → Location Maps → Rent Comps → Financial Analysis → Sales Comps → Market Overview
- Photography-heavy (aerial drone, ground-level)
- No hyperbole — the numbers speak

## Anti-Patterns (Always Avoid)
- Corporate filler ("synergies", "best-in-class", "leverage", "circle back")
- Opening emails with pleasantries before the actual point
- Vague claims without data to back them up
- Over-explaining what the reader already knows
- Passive voice where active is cleaner
